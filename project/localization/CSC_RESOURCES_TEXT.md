Flax is one of the oldest cultivated plants, with its use dating back over 30,000 years. Native to the Fertile Crescent, flax was domesticated for both its fibers and seeds. In ancient Egypt, linen woven from flax was prized for its smoothness and used in everything from everyday clothing to sacred burial shrouds. Meanwhile, flax seeds and their oil (linseed) were consumed and used in baking across many ancient cultures, including in Greek and Roman diets. Today, flax continues to bridge these worlds: its fibers are essential in sustainable textile production, while flaxseed flour and oil remain valued for their nutritional benefits in breads, crackers, and other baked goods.
